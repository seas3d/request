METERIAL SELECTION


VEROWHITE

Pros:

  • • 50μm resolution
  • • Fastest turn around time
  • • Biocompatible
  • • Great for molding silicone
  • • Great for molding microchannels
  • • Isotropic and non-porous

Cons:

  • • Very expensive
  • • Will creep overtime
  • • Brittle
  • • Non-structural
  • • Not UV stabilized
  • • Support material leaves matte finish

VEROBLUE

Pros:

  • • 50μm resolution
  • • Fastest turn around time
  • • Biocompatible
  • • Great for molding silicone
  • • Great for molding microchannels
  • • Isotropic and non-porous

Cons:

  • • Very expensive
  • • Will creep overtime
  • • Brittle
  • • Non-structural
  • • Not UV stabilized
  • • Support material leaves matte finish

VEROCLEAR

Pros:

  • • 26μm resolution in z-axis
  • • Fastest turn around time
  • • Biocompatible
  • • Translucent
  • • Great for molding silicone
  • • Great for molding microchannels
  • • Isotropic and non-porous

Cons:

  • • Very expensive
  • • Will creep overtime
  • • Brittle
  • • Non-structural
  • • Not UV stabilized
  • • Support material leaves matte finish

FDM PLA

Pros:

  • • Great for prototyping
  • • Most widely applicable
  • • Relativley inexpensive
  • • Structurally sound
  • • Does not creep
  • • UV stabile

Cons:

  • • Non-isotropic and porous
  • • Surface finish may vary

FDM ONYX

Pros:

  • • Production quality
  • • Widely applicable
  • • Structurally sound
  • • Does not creep
  • • UV stabile
  • • Does not fail catastrophically

Cons:

  • • Slow
  • • Non-isotropic and porous
  • • Expensive
  • • Increased likelyhood of print failure or defect

FORMLABS

Pros:

  • • Isotropic
  • • Production quality
  • • Structurally sound

Cons:

  • • Slowest
  • • Narrow use case
  • • Expensive
  • • Support structure leaves bumpy surface
  • • Even finished parts are sometimes sticky
